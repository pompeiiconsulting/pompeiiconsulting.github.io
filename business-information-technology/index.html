Business Basics

Pompeii Consulting | 18 Aug 2016

It is not from the benevolence of the butcher, the brewer, or the baker, that we expect our dinner, but from their regard to their own interest

Adam Smith, The Wealth of Nations

Businesses try to maximize profit
Profit, net income, net earnings, bottom line (of income statement)

Profit = Total Revenues - Total Costs
Profit = (Price x Quantity) - (Total Fixed Costs + Total Variable Costs)

To increase profit

  • increase total revenue
    • increase price
    • increase quantity
  • decrease total costs
    • decrease fixed costs
    • decrease variable costs

Law of Demand

Inverse relationship between price and quantity

Demand Side Economies of Scale

Violates the Law of Demand

  • Luxury Goods: as price increases so does quantity
    • Rolex, Gucci
  • Network Goods: as quantity increases so does price
    • Microsoft Office, Skype, Facebook

Supply Side Economics

Desire to decrease average fixed cost and average variable cost

Supply Side Economies of Scale

Increase quantity leads to a decrease in average costs

  • Average Fixed Costs = Total Fixed Costs / Quantity
    • Fixed cost leverage
  • Average Variable Costs = Total Variable Costs / Quantity
    • volume discount, learning effect, division of labor

Maximize Profit

Marginal Revenues = Marginal Costs

Information Technology

Impacts on Profitability

  • Network Effects
    • Winner take all systems
  • Software
    • large fixed cost
    • near zero variable costs
    • example: freeware
  • Information Technology enhanced learning
  • Information Technology based transformation of industry structure
    • example: Netflix vs Blockbuster

Business Ethics

  • Resources be used efficiently and allocated effectively
  • Greatest economic value can be created
