Importance of Information

Pompeii Consulting | 07 Sep 2016

Invisible Hand

By pursing his own interest, he frequently promotes that of the society more effectively than when he really intends to promote it... led by an invisible hand to promote an end which was no part of his intention

Adam Smith

  • conditions for perfectly competitive markets

    • many buyers and sellers who are price takers
    • products are homogeneous
    • there are no barriers to entry or exit
    • perfect information about actions is available (not just complete information abut payoff and preference)

  • perfectly competitive markets are only theoretically possible

Effects of Imperfect Information

  • game theory
  • prisoner's dilemma
  • choices
    • testify against the other
    • keep quiet
  • neither knows what the other chooses
  • low prison time if neither confess
  • no prison time if you're the one to confess and the other does not
  • medium prison time if you both confess
  • Nash equilibrium where each person confesses is the worst situation
  • Pareto optimal where neither confesses is the best situation
  • John Nash won a Nobel prize in 1994
  • proved the existence of a t least one equilibrium point for any non-cooperative non zero-sum game with any numbers of players
  • market and invisible hand fails because only imperfect information and not complete information is available when players are choosing an action
  • information technology is especially useful in addressing this problem
  • information technology makes it easier to share information about actions among agents
