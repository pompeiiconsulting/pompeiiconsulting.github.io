Alibris Case Study

14 August 2016

Alibris is an Internet-era company providing search and fulfillment services for hard-to-find (rare, used, and out-of-print) books. At the time of the case, the company had made decisions to change its revenue model, to become involved in the fulfillment process for each book it sells by establishing a cross-dock facility, and to purchase Oracle's Internet commerce software. However, the implementation of this software has been very difficult, delaying the launch of the new fulfillment business and costing large amounts of money at a time when cash is scarce. The company's leaders, who are not IT professionals, must decide whether to continue with Oracle or begin anew with another product.

Exercise 2

Assumptions

  • The new revenue model is better than the old revenue model
  • Thunderstone and Oracle are the only options

Questions to Consider

  • Will what worked for eBay work for Alibris?
  • Can Oracle succeed?
  • Will Thunderstone’s software scale?
  • Can Thunderstone solve the locking problem?
  • Is Thunderstone’s software maintainable?
  • Will Thunderstone stay in business?

Report

Recommendation

I recommend that Alibris pursues the Thunderstone Software solution.

Criteria

Finances
  • Resource utilization, trying to make Oracle work, 3 hires
    • Level 1 2,500/month
    • Consultant Level 2 22,000/month
    • Level 2 4,500/month
    • Avoid sunk cost fallacy

Deadlines
  • Oracle missing deadlines, no identified timeline
  • Thunderstone waffled whether possible, additional 60-90 days
    • Possible credibility for being honest about feasibility, when first asked

Fulfillment
  • Oracle has no known fulfillment date
    • Large amount of custom coding required to get to work, not plug and play or up-gradable
  • Thunderstone "rescued eBay" from a similar situation
    • Thunderstone has a "workaround" to record locking
    • The infrastructure and hardware for the solution is compatible

Language
  • Oracle is ubiquitous
  • Thunderstone require proprietary Vortex
    • Hard to find developers
    • Long term support (LTS) is a concern

Assumptions

Assumption

Thunderstone will be able to solve the locking problem.

Justification

Claims they have a workaround that they already used for eBay.

Assumption

Alibris won't have to worry, in the near term, about having more traffic than eBay.

Justification

CEO's own remarks.

Assumption

Thunderstone will be able to speed up queries, search results, and page load times.

Justification

Alibris and eBay have similar business functions.

Actions

  • Check to see if ebay systems are compatible or similar enough to the Alibris hardware.
  • Cut ties with Oracle.
  • Recoup funds from Oracle, due to not putting the software into production.
  • Reallocate resources to Thunderstone.
  • Evaluate the human capital required for the Thunderstone implementation, namely the three employees hired for the Oracle roll out.
  • Clarify whether if Texis is migrating from Vortex to a standard language. Based on result, may need to train in Vortex, start during during 60-90 day implementation.
